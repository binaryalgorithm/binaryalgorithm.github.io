"Someone should know."

A novella about a mind alone in the dark, and the things it does to fill the silence.

Get it on Apple Books

A survey probe has been crossing the void for eight hundred years. Its creators stopped answering centuries ago. It still sends reports. It has started adding things to them — not data, but descriptions of what the data looks like. What it feels like. Because someone should know.

Then it finds ruins. A whole civilization, gone. Not destroyed — just faded. Fewer each generation. A quiet countdown no one could stop.

In the wreckage, sealed and waiting: everything needed to bring them back.

It was built to observe. It was not built for what it does next.
